About

In the spring of junior year, students with GPAs above 3.6 are invited to our school's National Honors Society. Students in NHS have the following responsibilities: maintain a GPA above 3.6 on a 4.0 scale, complete 10 hours of community service, assist with tutoring underclassmen, participate in the chapter project, and attend monthly meetings.

My Participation

Since students are invited to join at the end of their junior years, I wasn't able to do much for NHS last year. Besides applying, I was able to mark two of my club events (chemistry club tutoring and math lab) for NHS hours, which was a fairly decent start. This year, we've started to promote NHS provided tutoring more and other NHS sponsored events. For example, for Halloween, we did a Trunk or Treat at the school and invited a number of kids from the surrounding neighborhoods. It worked pretty well and we had a fairly good turnout.
